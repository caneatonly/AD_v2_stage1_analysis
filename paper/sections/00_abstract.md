Underwater corner reflectors are widely used as passive, high-contrast markers for marine detection and localization. However, dedicated deployment platforms specifically designed for these devices are rarely reported in the open literature. This paper presents a mission-oriented, self-suspending underwater platform tailored for corner-reflector deployment. Based on the  physical realization of the platform, we specifically characterize its critical mission-defining transition: moving from a compact, near-horizontal release with a nonzero initial velocity to a stable, near-vertical working posture. To model this transitional phase, we develop an interpretable planar three-degree-of-freedom (3-DOF) dynamic model encompassing surge, heave, and pitch. The directional coupling between the rigid-body motion and the entrained water within the platform’s open-frame structure is captured by three bounded, anisotropic permeability-corrected inertial parameters. Static forces and moments are mapped by using CFD-derived coefficient tables to achieve hydrodynamic closure. Meanwhile, rotational damping and remaining dynamic parameters are identified through standardized free-decay experiments, employing strict condition-level data splitting to separate calibration from validation.
Validation against independent out-of-sample datasets demonstrates the model's high predictive accuracy, yielding a root-mean-square error (RMSE) of strictly less than [2.5°] for pitch and [0.15] rad/s for angular velocity. Leveraging this validated model, extensive parametric analyses reveal how initial deployment conditions govern the platform's transitional dynamics. Results show that the initial release angle significantly dominates the settling time and trajectory stability, whereas the initial surge velocity has a marginal effect on the final posture. Furthermore, initial release angles exceeding a critical threshold (e.g., [>30°] from horizontal) induce severe hydrodynamic overshoot, destabilizing the transition process. Ultimately, this study delineates a precise initial-condition feasibility envelope, providing practical, sensitivity-based operational guidance to ensure stable and efficient horizontal-to-vertical transitions for open-frame underwater platforms.
Keywords: Underwater platform; Mission-oriented deployment; Free-decay identification; CFD evidence; Added mass; Permeability correction
