## 4.1 Computational setup

The CFD geometry corresponds to the fairing-attached, reflector-uninflated transition-stage configuration described in Section 2. The full external wetted surface, including the nose fairing, tail cone, and thruster shroud, was retained in the computational model; small-scale features such as fasteners, cable passages, and sensor ports were suppressed to avoid excessive local mesh refinement without altering the global force and moment characteristics. All forces and moments were evaluated in the body-fixed coordinate frame defined in Section 3, with the origin located at the center of gravity.

The computational domain is a rectangular region extending [TBD]$L$ upstream, [TBD]$L$ downstream, and [TBD]$L$ in the lateral and vertical directions from the platform center, where $L = 0.625$ m is the overall body length. The platform was enclosed in a cylindrical overset region that is embedded within the stationary background domain through an overset mesh interface, as illustrated in Fig.~\ref{fig:cfd_domain}(a). A uniform velocity was prescribed at the inlet boundary, a pressure condition was imposed at the outlet, and symmetry conditions were applied to the remaining lateral boundaries. The working fluid was water with density $\rho = 997.561$ kg/m$^3$ and dynamic viscosity $\mu = 8.8871 \times 10^{-4}$ Pa$\cdot$s.

The overset region was discretized with polyhedral cells and prism layers on the body surface to resolve the boundary layer, while the background domain employed polyhedral cells without prism layers. Fig.~\ref{fig:cfd_domain}(b) shows the surface mesh on the platform, and Fig.~\ref{fig:mesh_xoz} presents a cross-sectional view of the mesh in the $xOz$ plane, showing the overset--background interface and the prism-layer distribution near the body wall. The incompressible Reynolds-averaged Navier--Stokes equations were solved using the segregated flow solver with second-order spatial discretization. Turbulence was modeled by the SST $k$-$\omega$ model combined with the all-$y^+$ wall treatment, which blends wall-function and near-wall formulations according to the local mesh resolution. On the production mesh, the area-averaged wall $y^+$ on the platform surface was 7.91, with approximately 98\% of the surface area satisfying $y^+ < 30$. The medium mesh, comprising 8.22 million cells, was adopted for all production simulations after a targeted mesh-independence assessment at a representative high-load condition; the detailed comparison is reported in Section 4.5.

The computational setup described above is shared by the two CFD campaigns reported in Sections 4.2 and 4.3, which differ primarily in the flow condition imposed and the quantity extracted from the simulation.
